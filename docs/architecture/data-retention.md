# PROM data retention and deletion policy

Effective date: 2026-07-16

This policy applies to Projects, Service Desk, Access Service, shared file
storage, audit records, and outbox records. A legal hold always suspends
destruction and anonymization. No database relationship may erase business
history through an implicit cascade.

## Retention matrix

| Data class | Delete model | Retention | Archive/anonymization | Cascade and cleanup |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Projects and project tasks | Archive, then soft delete | 7 years after final archive | User-facing personal fields are anonymized when the account retention expires; project facts and state history remain | Child tasks and responses are retained with the project; no implicit hard cascade |
| Project responses | Soft delete | 7 years after project archive | Applicant display data may be pseudonymized; decision and timestamps remain | Attachments follow the response file policy |
| Service Desk tickets, comments, approvals, SLA and routing history | Soft close/archive; no ordinary hard delete | 7 years after ticket closure | Requester/participant display fields may be pseudonymized after account erasure; operational facts remain | Comments, approvals and SLA history never disappear through parent cascade |
| Published templates and catalog revisions | Immutable revision plus archive | 7 years after the last active revision | No anonymization unless free text contains personal data | Archived revisions remain addressable by historical tickets |
| Platform identities, memberships and role assignments | Disable immediately; hard-delete only after dependent history is anonymized | 1 year after account closure, unless business/legal hold requires longer | Replace direct profile attributes with a stable pseudonymous subject; retain external subject hash and audit references | Role/membership rows are revoked transactionally; business records keep pseudonymous actor IDs |
| Access and administrative audit events | Append-only | 7 years | Structured before/after data is minimized at write time; subject display fields may be pseudonymized | Never cascaded from users, roles, modules or business objects |
| Delivered outbox events | Hard delete in batches | 30 days after successful publication | Event payload must already be data-minimized | Failed/dead-letter records retain diagnostics for 90 days, then purge unless under investigation |
| User notifications | Hard delete in batches | 1 year after creation | No archive | Notification deletion does not delete its ticket/project |
| Application logs and request traces | Time-based destruction | 30 days in production; 7 days in non-production | Secrets, tokens and file contents are prohibited at ingestion | Storage lifecycle policy performs deletion |
| Uploaded files | Quarantine, active, then owner-bound retention | Same period as owner; rejected quarantine files: 7 days | Malware verdict, checksum and audit metadata remain for 1 year after blob deletion | Blob deletion is explicit and idempotent; metadata is marked deleted only after storage confirms removal |
| Orphaned upload blobs | Hard delete | Within 24 hours of detection | Keep checksum, reason and cleanup result for 30 days | Cleanup scans storage keys against committed attachment metadata |
| Backups | Encrypted rotation | 35 daily copies plus 12 monthly copies | Restore access is audited | Expired backup media is cryptographically destroyed |

## Deletion workflow

1. Validate actor permission, legal hold, object state and version.
2. Write the domain change, append-only audit event and outbox event in one
   database transaction.
3. For files, enqueue an idempotent storage cleanup operation; never delete the
   blob before the database transaction commits.
4. A worker deletes eligible rows/blobs in bounded batches and records counts,
   failures and retry state without high-cardinality metric labels.
5. Reconciliation detects orphan blobs, metadata without blobs, stale
   quarantine objects and outbox rows outside their retention window.

## File-specific guarantees

- Filename, media type, declared size and actual size are validated.
- Storage keys are generated by the server and cannot contain user paths.
- A checksum is computed before activation.
- The file is unavailable to normal download while quarantined or rejected.
- Scanner failure is fail-closed in production: the file remains quarantined.
- Download authorization is re-evaluated against the owning object.
- Cleanup is retry-safe and records an audit event for sensitive operations.

## Implementation ownership

- Each product owns retention queries for its database.
- The platform SDK owns reusable batch, outbox and storage primitives, not
  product-specific retention periods.
- Operations owns backup and log lifecycle policies.
- Security/legal approval is required to shorten a period or expand retained
  personal data.

The migration is not complete until scheduled cleanup jobs, PostgreSQL tests,
orphan reconciliation and audit/outbox assertions enforce this document.
